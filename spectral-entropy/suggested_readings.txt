================================================================================
SUGGESTED READINGS & BIBTEX ITEMS FOR SPECTRAL ENTROPY OF MODULAR MULTIPLICATION
================================================================================

This document collects the foundational suggested readings spanning number theory,
information theory, complex analysis, and quantum mechanics that underpin the
theoretic and numerical results of this paper. Each reading is accompanied by a
brief description of its relevance and its corresponding BibTeX citation item.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. FINITE FIELDS & NUMBER THEORY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ireland, K., & Rosen, M. (1990). "A Classical Introduction to Modern Number Theory"
- Relevance: Provides the foundational number-theoretic background, including properties
  of finite fields, cyclic groups of units, and the Riemann Zeta function / Von Mangoldt.
- BibTeX:
@book{ireland_rosen,
  title={A Classical Introduction to Modern Number Theory},
  author={Ireland, Kenneth and Rosen, Michael},
  volume={84},
  year={1990},
  publisher={Springer Science \& Business Media},
  edition={2nd}
}

Lidl, R., & Niederreiter, H. (1997). "Finite Fields"
- Relevance: The definitive reference on the algebraic structure of finite fields, row
  permutations, and modular arithmetic distributions.
- BibTeX:
@book{lidl_niederreiter,
  title={Finite Fields},
  author={Lidl, Rudolf and Niederreiter, Harald},
  volume={20},
  year={1997},
  publisher={Cambridge University Press},
  edition={2nd}
}

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. INFORMATION THEORY & SHANNON ENTROPY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Shannon, C. E. (1948). "A Mathematical Theory of Communication"
- Relevance: The original, seminal paper establishing Shannon entropy, coding capacity,
  and the properties of uniform distributions.
- BibTeX:
@article{shannon1948,
  title={A mathematical theory of communication},
  author={Shannon, Claude Elwood},
  journal={Bell System Technical Journal},
  volume={27},
  number={3},
  pages={379--423},
  year={1948},
  publisher={Nokia Bell Labs}
}

Cover, T. M., & Thomas, J. A. (2006). "Elements of Information Theory"
- Relevance: Provides rigorous mathematical proofs for the properties of Shannon entropy,
  including strict concavity and maximization conditions over finite probability spaces.
- BibTeX:
@book{cover_thomas,
  title={Elements of Information Theory},
  author={Cover, Thomas M and Thomas, Joy A},
  year={2006},
  publisher={John Wiley \& Sons},
  edition={2nd}
}

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. COMPLEX ANALYSIS & INTEGRAL TRANSFORMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Garnett, J. B. (2007). "Bounded Analytic Functions"
- Relevance: Contains the theory of Cauchy transforms and Stieltjes integrals on the
  boundary of the unit circle, framing the meromorphic behavior of S_n(z).
- BibTeX:
@book{garnett,
  title={Bounded Analytic Functions},
  author={Garnett, John B},
  volume={236},
  year={2007},
  publisher={Springer Science \& Business Media},
  edition={Revised 1st}
}

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. QUANTUM MECHANICS & BAND STRUCTURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Kronig, R. de L., & Penney, W. G. (1931). "Quantum Mechanics of Electrons in Crystal Lattices"
- Relevance: The foundational model for electrons in periodic delta potentials, serving
  as the direct physical mapping for prime rings on the unit circle.
- BibTeX:
@article{kronig_penney,
  title={Quantum mechanics of electrons in crystal lattices},
  author={Kronig, R de L and Penney, WG},
  journal={Proceedings of the Royal Society of London. Series A, Containing Papers of a Mathematical and Physical Character},
  volume={130},
  number={814},
  pages={499--513},
  year={1931},
  publisher={The Royal Society London}
}

Griffiths, D. J., & Schroeter, D. F. (2018). "Introduction to Quantum Mechanics"
- Relevance: Introductory quantum textbook detailing periodic delta potentials, Bloch
  wavefunctions, and Schrödinger ring boundary conditions.
- BibTeX:
@book{griffiths_qm,
  title={Introduction to Quantum Mechanics},
  author={Griffiths, David J and Schroeter, Darrell F},
  year={2018},
  publisher={Cambridge University Press},
  edition={3rd}
}

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. RANDOM MATRIX THEORY, PRIMES, & QUANTUM CHAOS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Montgomery, H. L. (1973). "The Pair Correlation of Zeros of the Zeta Function"
- Relevance: The landmark paper connecting the statistical spacing of Riemann Zeta zeros
  to eigenvalues of random Hermitian matrices (GUE).
- BibTeX:
@inproceedings{montgomery1973,
  title={The pair correlation of zeros of the zeta function},
  author={Montgomery, Hugh L},
  booktitle={Analytic Number Theory},
  volume={24},
  pages={181--193},
  year={1973},
  organization={American Mathematical Society}
}

Keating, J. P., & Snaith, N. C. (2000). "Random Matrix Theory and \zeta(1/2+it)"
- Relevance: Explores the deeper connection between random matrix ensembles, characteristic
  polynomials, and the value distribution of number-theoretic zeta functions.
- BibTeX:
@article{keating_snaith,
  title={Random Matrix Theory and $\backslash$zeta (1/2+ it)},
  author={Keating, Jonathan P and Snaith, Nina C},
  journal={Communications in Mathematical Physics},
  volume={214},
  number={1},
  pages={57--89},
  year={2000},
  publisher={Springer}
}

Berry, M. V., & Keating, J. P. (1999). "The Riemann Zeros and Eigenvalue Asymptotics"
- Relevance: Outlines the physical background of the Hilbert-Pólya conjecture, mapping
  zeros of zeta functions to physical Hamiltonian energy levels.
- BibTeX:
@article{berry_keating,
  title={The Riemann zeros and eigenvalue asymptotics},
  author={Berry, Michael V and Keating, Jonathan P},
  journal={SIAM Review},
  volume={41},
  number={2},
  pages={236--266},
  year={1999},
  publisher={SIAM}
}

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. DISORDERED SYSTEMS & LOCALIZATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Anderson, P. W. (1958). "Absence of Diffusion in Certain Random Lattices"
- Relevance: The classic paper establishing Anderson localization, which explains the
  collapse of wavefunctions (delocalization to localization transition) in amorphous
  lattices like the composite ring.
- BibTeX:
@article{anderson1958,
  title={Absence of diffusion in certain random lattices},
  author={Anderson, Philip W},
  journal={Physical Review},
  volume={109},
  number={5},
  pages={1492},
  year={1958},
  publisher={APS}
}
